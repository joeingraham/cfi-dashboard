MAU Growth by Month i
Purpose: Decomposes monthly MAU change into activations, resurrections, and churn. The net line shows whether growth is accelerating.

Activations i
Purpose: Toggle between activations by plan tier (SKU) or acquisition channel. Shows where new users come from and what plans they land on.

Purpose: Monthly conversion rates for upgrades and downgrades across plan tiers. Upward upgrade curves + declining downgrades = healthy plan expansion.

Finance

Revenue, cost, margins, and unit economics

Subscription vs Overage i
Purpose: Split between base subscription revenue and usage-based overage charges.
📌 Overage growing as share of total — usage-based pricing working.
(Illustrative Data)
Revenue, COGS & Gross Margin i
Purpose: Revenue above x-axis, COGS below. GM% line on right axis shows margin trajectory.
📌 COGS growing faster than revenue — gross margins are deeply negative (~-41% to -69%) and worsening as usage scales.
(Illustrative Data)
Gross Profit by SKU i
Purpose: Per-SKU profitability. Negative = SKU costs more to serve than it earns. Stacked bar shows which SKUs subsidize others.
📌 All SKUs have negative GP — COGS exceed revenue across every tier. Business has the largest absolute deficit.
(Illustrative Data)
Gross Profit by Usage Percentile i
Purpose: GP per user segmented by usage intensity. Shows which usage tiers are most costly — heavy users drive disproportionate COGS.
📌 Top 1% of users (P99-100) are deeply negative at -$85/user/mo. Bottom 50% are near breakeven. Usage intensity is the key margin driver.
(Illustrative Data)
Monthly Revenue Bridge i
Purpose: Decomposes monthly revenue change into net new users, expansion (upgrades/overage), contraction (downgrades), and churn. Net line should tie to the monthly revenue increase visible in other charts.
📌 Net monthly revenue growing $11M→$22M/mo. Expansion overtaking net new as the primary growth driver by month 8.
(Illustrative Data)
ARPU by SKU i
Purpose: Revenue per user by plan. Positive values.
📌 Enterprise highest at ~$47/mo. All paid SKUs trending up.
(Illustrative Data)
COGS per User by SKU i
Purpose: Cost to serve each user. Shown as negative values for visual comparison with ARPU.
📌 COGSPU rising across all SKUs as usage intensity grows. Pro+ highest at ~$33-45/user/mo. Free at ~$0.40-0.97.
(Illustrative Data)
Gross Profit per User by SKU i
Purpose: ARPU minus COGS per user. Positive = profitable, negative = subsidized. The unit economics truth.
📌 All SKUs show negative unit economics — COGSPU exceeds ARPU. Enterprise least negative; Pro+ most underwater.
(Illustrative Data)

Utilization & Engagement

Stickiness, engagement depth, usage patterns, and surface adoption

WAU / MAU Stickiness i
Purpose: Weekly-to-monthly active user ratio. Shows what share of MAU return weekly. Higher = stickier product.
📌 WAU/MAU at ~74% is excellent — 3 in 4 monthly users come back every week. Well above the 50% goal.
(Illustrative Data)
DAU / WAU Stickiness i
Purpose: Daily-to-weekly active user ratio. Measures daily habit formation among weekly users.
📌 DAU/WAU reaching 63% — daily usage habit is strengthening. On track for the 60% goal.
(Illustrative Data)
Usage Frequency Distribution i
Purpose: Shows % of MAU by days active per week. Healthy trends show shift from 0–2 days toward 5–7 days.
📌 7-day users grew from 4% to 10% of MAU — power user segment is expanding. 0-day (lapsed) users declining.
(Illustrative Data)
Interactions per User per Day i
Purpose: Average interactions (completions, chat turns, CLI commands, etc.) per active user per day across all surfaces.
📌 Interactions nearly doubled from 12 to 22/day — users are engaging more deeply across all product surfaces.
(Illustrative Data)
MAU by Product Surface i
Purpose: Which product features have traction? Compares MAU across Completions, Chat, CLI, PR Summaries, Agent.
📌 Completions still dominates at 2.1M but Chat (1.4M) is closing fast — the shift to conversational AI is real.
(Illustrative Data)
Multi-Surface Users i
Purpose: Multi-surface users are stickier and harder to churn. 3+ surface adoption = platform lock-in.
📌 3+ surface users grew from 8% to 28% — strong platform stickiness trend.
(Illustrative Data)
Usage Share by Product Surface i
Purpose: Tracks the shift from inline completions to conversational AI. Is Chat cannibalizing or complementing?
📌 Chat grew from 25% to 40% of usage mix. Evidence suggests complementary — total usage is growing alongside the mix shift.
(Illustrative Data)
M12 Retention by # of Surfaces i
Purpose: 12-month retention by number of product surfaces used. More surfaces = stickier users.
📌 4+ surface users retain at 79% vs 47% for single-surface. Each additional surface adds ~10pp retention.
(Illustrative Data)

Retention & Health

Cohort retention and return rates

Return Rate & User Base i
Purpose: Shows cumulative activations, current MAU, returning users, and return rate on dual axes. Tracks user base health and re-engagement.
📌 Return rate at 42% — less than half of ever-activated users are currently active. But trending up slightly. 6.2M ever activated, 2.6M currently active.
(Illustrative Data)
User Retention J-Curves by Cohort i
Purpose: Monthly user retention curves for each cohort. J-curve shape = initial churn then stabilization. Newer cohorts above older = improving product.
📌 Recent cohorts show higher floor retention — onboarding improvements are working.
(Illustrative Data)
Revenue Retention J-Curves by Cohort i
Purpose: Monthly revenue retention curves for each cohort. Shows how cohort revenue holds up over time — values >100% indicate expansion revenue.
📌 Recent cohorts show improving revenue retention — upsells and overage are offsetting churn-driven revenue loss.
(Illustrative Data)
Retention Milestones Over Time i
Purpose: Tracks M1, M3, M6, M12 retention rates over time. Are retention milestones improving?
📌 M1 retention improved from 72% to 81%. M12 retention steady at ~58% — long-term stickiness holding.
(Illustrative Data)
Cohorted Revenue Over Time i
Purpose: Total revenue trajectory by cohort month.
📌 Newer cohorts ramping faster — product-led growth improving.
(Illustrative Data)
Cohorted Revenue per User i
Purpose: Revenue per user by cohort month. Shows if per-user monetization is improving.
📌 Newer cohorts generating higher ARPU — pricing and overage adoption improving.
(Illustrative Data)
Cohorted COGS Over Time i
Purpose: Total COGS trajectory by cohort month. Declines as users churn, but remaining users are heavier.
📌 Total COGS per cohort declines as users churn. Newer cohorts start higher due to larger intake and heavier usage.
(Illustrative Data)
Cohorted COGS per User i
Purpose: COGS per remaining user by cohort month. Increases as churned users were lighter — survivors are power users.
📌 COGS/user rises within each cohort as light users churn. Newer cohorts start lower — efficiency is improving.
(Illustrative Data)
